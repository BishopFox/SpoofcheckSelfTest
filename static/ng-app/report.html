Recommendations

To avoid the risk of email spoofing from {{domain}}, Bishop Fox recommends the following:

  • Begin implementing an SPF record for {{domain}}. SPF records are DNS TXT records on the root {{domain}} domain that define all of the IP addresses permitted to send emails from {{domain}}. Additional information on setting up SPF records can be found at the OpenSPF project's SPF Record Syntax page.
  • Modify the SPF record for {{domain}} to contain a reject all mechanism.This is performed by adding the string -all or ~all to the SPF record for {{domain}}. Additional information on setting up SPF records can be found at the OpenSPF project's SPF Record Syntax page.
  • Begin implementing a DMARC record for {{domain}}. DMARC records are DNS TXT records located at the _dmarc.{{domain}} subdomain that instruct receiving mail servers on how to handle emails that fail SPF and DKIM alignment. For DMARC to function, {{domain}} needs to have both SPF and DKIM configured. Additional information about setting up DMARC records can be found from the Google Apps DMARC setup guide.
  • A DMARC policy of none allows spoofed emails to be delivered. Begin implementing a DMARC policy of quarantine or reject. As implementing strict DMARC policies may interfere with the deliver of email from {{domain}} email addresses, Bishop Fox recommends setting up and monitoring aggregate report notifications for legitimate emails before beginning to implement a stricter policy. If no legitimate emails are reported, set the DMARC policy to quarantine and set the pct field to a low percentage. This process is described in more detail in the Google Apps DMARC setup guide.
